Global Warming 🌎

Global Warming Definition:

"Global warming refers to the gradual rise in Earth's temperature, primarily brought about by the greenhouse effect, which is exacerbated by elevated concentrations of carbon dioxide, CFCs, and other pollutants."

What is Global Warming ❓

Global warming is the gradual increase in temperature near the Earth's surface. This behavior has been seen for the last one or two centuries. This alteration has altered the earth's climatic pattern. However, the concept of global warming is extremely controversial, yet scientists have presented important data to support the fact that the Earth's temperature is constantly rising. Global warming is caused by a variety of factors that are harmful to humans, plants, and animals. These factors could be natural or the result of human efforts. Understanding the harmful effects of global warming is critical for addressing the challenges.

Causes of Global Warming 😑

Following are the major causes of global warming:

Man-Made Causes of Global Warming

Deforestation:🌲
Plants are essential for providing oxygen. They absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen, keeping the environment balanced. However, forests are being cut down for various purposes, leading to an imbalance and contributing to global warming.

Use of Vehicles: 🚙
Driving vehicles, even for short trips, releases gases into the air. Vehicles burn fossil fuels, which produce a lot of carbon dioxide and other pollutants, increasing the Earth's temperature.

Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs):
Using air conditioners and refrigerators releases CFCs into the air. These chemicals harm the ozone layer, which protects us from the sun's harmful ultraviolet rays. Damage to the ozone layer allows more UV rays to reach the Earth, raising its temperature.

Natural Causes of Global Warming

Volcanoes:🌋
Volcanoes are a major natural cause of global warming. When they erupt, the ash and smoke released into the atmosphere can affect the climate.

Water Vapor:
Water vapor is a greenhouse gas. As the Earth's temperature rises, more water evaporates from oceans and lakes, staying in the atmosphere and adding to global warming.

Melting Permafrost:
Permafrost is frozen soil that traps gases. Found under the Earth's surface, especially in glaciers, it releases these gases when it melts, which increases the Earth's temperature.

Forest Fires: 🔥
Forest fires release a lot of carbon-rich smoke into the air. These gases contribute to global warming by raising the Earth's temperature.

Effects of Global Warming

Rise in Temperature: 🌡️
Global warming has caused a significant increase in Earth's temperature. Since 1880, the temperature has risen by about 1 degree Celsius. This warming has accelerated glacier melting, which raises sea levels and threatens coastal areas.

Threats to the Ecosystem:
Global warming negatively impacts coral reefs, leading to the loss of plant and animal life. The rising global temperatures have made these delicate ecosystems even more vulnerable.

Climate Change:
Global warming has altered weather patterns, causing droughts in some areas and floods in others. This imbalance in climate conditions is a direct result of global warming.

Spread of Diseases: 🤒
Global warming changes heat and humidity patterns, which helps mosquitoes and other disease-carrying insects to spread to new areas, increasing the risk of illnesses.
